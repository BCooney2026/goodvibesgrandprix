What On Earth Is This

A Race Like No Other

Part vintage speed-shop spectacle, part charity throwdown, all good vibes. Our field of, ahem, self-propelled novelty racers buzz down the track under their own power while the crowd bids, cheers, and roars. It's cheeky, it's loud, and every dollar drives toward a serious cause. Bring your competitive streak and a sense of humor. Leave the kids at home.

100% of net proceeds benefit Mom's House · Phoenixville →
Why We Race

For Mom's House

A hand up, not a handout, since 1987

Mom's House of Phoenixville provides free, state-licensed childcare so single, low-income parents can finish school and build a career. They receive zero government funding; every dollar comes from donors and neighbors. That's where race night comes in.

The Format

How The Race Runs

01

A Full Card Of Races

A night of heats, four racers per race, each one lined up on the track and ready to buzz. Sponsor ducks, business racers, and public entries all take their turn.

02

Back A Racer To Win

Pick the racer you think takes the heat and put money on it. Sponsored races run auction-style; the individual races run on $5 backing tickets. Back a winner and you take home part of the pot.

03

They're Off

Loud music, confetti, the crack of the gong, the racers vibrate their way to the finish line. Winner takes the glory.

The Finale: Slowest Wins

We flip the script for the last race of the night. Dead last across the line takes the crown. Anything can happen, and usually does.

How The Money Works

Two ways to put money on a racer, depending on the race. Either way, half the pot goes to Mom's House.

Back A Racer

$5 per ticket · Individual races

In the individual races, buy $5 tickets on the racer you think wins. When the race is over, the whole pot for that race splits down the middle.

Half the pot splits among everyone holding tickets on the winning racer. The other half goes to Mom's House. More tickets on the winner you hold, the bigger your share.

Bid In The Auction

Sponsored races

The sponsored races (Grand Prix ducks and Checkered Flag racers) run auction-style. Back your favorite from the floor and cheer it home.

Every dollar drives toward the cause. It's the loudest, most competitive part of the night, and it's all for Mom's House.

Race Your Own Racer — $20

Don't just watch, get in the race. Bring your own novelty racer, brand new in the box, and drop it off at the Post at least a week before race night to get it cleared and named. Then watch it fly (or crawl) down the track with your name on it.

You line up against three other racers. Each of the four pays $20 to enter, so the race carries an $80 pot. Win the heat and you take $40; the other $40 goes to Mom's House.

The $20 entry is separate from the $5 backing tickets, and it does not include your door admission. Simple rules apply, we'll walk you through them at drop-off.

$20 Cash at Drop-Off · Cash Only

Pit Wall Questions

FAQ

Okay, what are the "racers," really?
Let's not be coy about it: the racers are novelty vibrators. Yes, those. It's the same wildly popular charity format that packs houses in Key West, and it turns out a room full of buzzing little racers tearing down a track is one of the funniest things you'll ever cheer for. The humor lives in the racing, the vintage duck, the checkered flags, the photo finish. It's cheeky and it's a blast, and it's all in good fun for a serious cause. Come with a sense of humor.
Wait, is this a real thing?
Completely. It's a proven charity format made famous in Key West, where it sells out weeks in advance. Ours is the first annual Pennsylvania edition, conducted by Phoenixville VFW Post 1564, with net proceeds going to Mom's House of Phoenixville. First year is the one people brag about being at.
How do I get in on the racing?
Three ways. Back a racer: in the individual races, buy $5 tickets on the racer you think wins, and split the pot if it does. In the sponsored races, bid auction-style from the floor. Enter your own racer for $20 (cash, at drop-off), brand new in the box, dropped at the Post about a week ahead so we can clear it and give it a race name, just twelve public spots exist. Or sponsor a Grand Prix Duck or a Checkered Flag racer as a local business. See How It Works and Sponsors.
Can I bring my own racer?
Yes! Public entrants ($20) and Checkered Flag sponsors ($100) bring their own novelty racer, dropped at the Post at least seven days before race night so it can be inspected and cleared. The one difference: public racers stay brand-new and sealed in the box, while Checkered Flag sponsors decorate theirs, so they bring it brand-new in its original packaging (unsealed is fine) with a receipt. A few simple rules keep the racing fair for everyone (self-powered, fits the lane, nothing unsafe), and we'll walk you through them at drop-off. Grand Prix sponsors are the exception: we provide those four ducks.
Why 21+?
It's an adult-humor event held in a licensed club with cash gaming. No exceptions, and photo ID gets checked at the door, yes, even if you have gray hair and grandkids.
What does it cost to get in?
Admission is $10 cash at the door. That gets you in for the whole night of racing. Backing a racer ($5 tickets in the individual races) and entering your own racer ($20) are separate, and cash only. Advance seating details will post right here as we finalize them, seating is limited, so grab the date on your calendar from the Details section.
Do I have to back a racer?
Nobody's forcing you, but this is not a spectator sport. In the individual races you back a racer with $5 tickets, pick a winner and you split the pot. In the sponsored races you bid from the floor. It's the whole fun, and every dollar drives to Mom's House. Bring cash; you'll want it when the racing heats up.
Where does the money actually go?
Net proceeds go to Mom's House of Phoenixville: free, state-licensed childcare so single parents can finish school. They receive zero government funding. Every dollar you spend backing a racer keeps the lights on.
Food? Drinks? Parking?
It's a VFW post, you will not go hungry or thirsty. Full details on food, drink, and parking will be posted along with ticketing info.
