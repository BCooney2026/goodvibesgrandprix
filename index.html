What On Earth Is This

A Race Like No Other

Part vintage speed-shop spectacle, part charity throwdown, all good vibes. Our field of, ahem, self-propelled novelty racers buzz down the track under their own power while the crowd bids, cheers, and roars. It's cheeky, it's loud, and every dollar drives toward a serious cause. Bring your competitive streak and a sense of humor. Leave the kids at home.

100% of net proceeds benefit Mom's House · Phoenixville →
Why We Race

For Mom's House

A hand up, not a handout, since 1987

Mom's House of Phoenixville provides free, state-licensed childcare so single, low-income parents can finish school and build a career. They receive zero government funding; every dollar comes from donors and neighbors. That's where race day comes in.

The Format

How The Race Runs

01

Six Races, Four Racers

Six heats across the day, four uniquely-named racers per heat, each one lined up on the track and ready to buzz.

02

Bid To Back A Racer

Before each heat, racers go up for auction, bidding opens at $10. Back your favorite, then watch it run.

03

They're Off

Loud music, confetti, the crack of the gong, the racers vibrate their way to the finish line. Winner takes the glory.

The Finale: Slowest Wins

We flip the script for the last race of the day. Dead last across the line takes the crown. Anything can happen, and usually does.

The Field

Meet The Racers

Twenty-four racers. Six heats. Zero dignity. Four names drop between now and race day, then the rest of the field stays a surprise until the green flag.

Pit Wall Questions

FAQ

Wait, is this real?
Extremely. It's a proven charity format made famous in Key West, where it sells out months in advance. Ours is the first annual Pennsylvania edition, run by VFW Post 1564 with 100% of net proceeds going to Mom's House of Phoenixville.
What exactly is racing?
Let's call them self-propelled novelty racers and leave the rest to your imagination, which is exactly where it belongs. It's cheeky, it's ridiculous, and it's all for the kids and parents at Mom's House. The innuendo stays on the track; the event itself is all laughs.
Why 21+?
It's an adult-humor event held in a licensed club with cash gaming. No exceptions, and photo ID gets checked at the door, yes, even if you have gray hair and grandkids.
How do tickets work?
Advance tickets guarantee your seat, and seating is limited. Ticketing details drop soon, right here on this site. Grab the date on your calendar from the Details section so you don't miss the drop.
Do I have to bid?
Nobody's forcing you, but this is not a spectator sport. Bidding starts at just $10, backing a racer is the whole fun, and every dollar drives to Mom's House. Bring cash; you'll want it when the bidding war starts.
Can I bring my own racer?
Absolutely not, and we can't believe you asked. All racers are provided, inspected, and named by the house, a level playing field is sacred in motorsport.
Where does the money actually go?
100% of net proceeds go to Mom's House of Phoenixville: free, state-licensed childcare so single parents can finish school. They receive zero government funding. Your bids literally keep the lights on.
Food? Drinks? Parking?
It's a VFW post, you will not go hungry or thirsty. Full details on food, drink, and parking will be posted along with ticketing info.
